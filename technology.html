Technology & engineering

An engineering-first approach: minimize complexity, maximize field usability, and design for scalable, modular deployment.

Design philosophy

NITROCOOL is built around mechanical simplicity: a compact pressurized cartridge, a controlled expansion pathway, and a delivery method that targets the skin efficiently—without electrical power.

Why mechanical simplicity matters

  • Reliability: fewer components, fewer failure modes.
  • Speed: minimal setup, fast activation.
  • Field readiness: no batteries, no charging cycles.

Modularity & scalability

The system concept supports different deployment needs, from individual use to team/crew kits—while keeping the core thermodynamic principle consistent.

Engineering focuses (high level)

Control

Repeatable discharge behavior and predictable cooling output.

Heat transfer

Maximize skin-zone cooling per cartridge use.

Ergonomics

Portable form factor with practical handling and storage.

Safety

Externally vented CO₂, controlled pathways, and clear usage guidance.

Research direction (without revealing IP)

Enough detail to show seriousness—without disclosing proprietary specifics.

Cooling pathway optimization

Geometry, restriction characteristics, and flow management to balance temperature drop, duration, and comfort.

Target-zone delivery

Attachment and interface concepts to improve heat extraction from high-impact body zones.

Field-ready packaging

Durability, portability, and simple operation for athletes, workers, and responders.

Measurement & validation

IR temperature mapping, skin temp delta, perceived exertion, and safety/comfort testing.
